Retiring on $1 million might sound impossible in a high-cost city like San Francisco or New York — but across the globe, a seven-figure portfolio can stretch remarkably far. The key is understanding the **4% rule** and choosing the right destination.

## How the 4% Rule Sets Your Budget

The safe withdrawal rate of 4% means a $1M portfolio generates roughly **$40,000 per year**, or about **$3,333 per month**. In many U.S. metro areas that barely covers rent, but in dozens of international cities it funds a genuinely comfortable lifestyle.

| Portfolio Size | Annual Withdrawal (4%) | Monthly Budget |
|---|---|---|
| $1,000,000 | $40,000 | $3,333 |
| $2,000,000 | $80,000 | $6,667 |
| $3,000,000 | $120,000 | $10,000 |
| $5,000,000 | $200,000 | $16,667 |

The question isn't whether $3,333/month is "enough" — it's **where** that amount buys you the life you want. Below are ten cities where it goes the furthest.

## Top 10 Cities Where $1M Goes the Furthest

### 1. [Chiang Mai, Thailand](/cities/chiang-mai)

**Monthly cost: $1,200–$1,800** · **Surplus: $1,500+/mo**

Chiang Mai consistently tops FIRE destination lists for good reason. A one-bedroom apartment in the Old City runs $300–$500/month. Street food meals cost $1–$2. The city offers excellent healthcare at a fraction of Western prices, a thriving digital nomad community, and stunning temples and nature at your doorstep.

At $3,333/month you'll live well and **reinvest over $1,500 every month** — letting your portfolio continue to grow even in retirement.

### 2. [Lisbon, Portugal](/cities/lisbon)

**Monthly cost: $2,000–$2,800** · **Surplus: $500–$1,300/mo**

Portugal's D7 visa is tailor-made for retirees with passive income. Lisbon offers world-class food, reliable public transit, and a mild Mediterranean climate. Healthcare through the national system is affordable and high-quality. The city has become a magnet for remote workers and retirees from across Europe and North America.

### 3. [Medellín, Colombia](/cities/medellin)

**Monthly cost: $1,400–$2,000** · **Surplus: $1,300+/mo**

Known as the "City of Eternal Spring" for its year-round 72°F weather, Medellín has transformed into a modern, safe metropolis. The Poblado neighborhood offers upscale living at budget prices, and the metro system is among the best in Latin America. Colombia's retirement visa requires just $750/month in pension or investment income.

### 4. [Da Nang, Vietnam](/cities/da-nang)

**Monthly cost: $1,000–$1,500** · **Surplus: $1,800+/mo**

Beachfront living on a budget. Da Nang sits between Hue and Hoi An on Vietnam's central coast. Modern apartments with ocean views rent for $400–$600/month. The food scene is incredible and incredibly cheap. This is one of the few places where you can live *on the beach* and still save more than half your monthly budget.

### 5. [Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia](/cities/kuala-lumpur)

**Monthly cost: $1,500–$2,200** · **Surplus: $1,100+/mo**

Malaysia's MM2H visa program welcomes retirees. KL offers a cosmopolitan lifestyle with excellent infrastructure, English widely spoken, and some of the best food in Asia. Modern condos with pools and gyms start at $500/month. The city is also a major flight hub, making regional travel easy and cheap.

### 6. [Mexico City, Mexico](/cities/mexico-city)

**Monthly cost: $1,600–$2,400** · **Surplus: $900+/mo**

A world-class capital with incredible culture, food, and nightlife. Neighborhoods like Roma Norte and Condesa offer walkable urban living with tree-lined streets and sidewalk cafés. Direct flights to most U.S. cities make it easy to visit family, and Mexico's temporary resident visa is straightforward for retirees.

### 7. [Budapest, Hungary](/cities/budapest)

**Monthly cost: $1,500–$2,200** · **Surplus: $1,100+/mo**

One of Europe's most affordable capitals. Thermal baths, ruin bars, and Danube views come standard. Hungary's flat 15% income tax rate is competitive, and the city punches well above its weight in culture and architecture. You get a genuinely European lifestyle at a fraction of Western European costs.

### 8. [George Town, Malaysia](/cities/george-town)

**Monthly cost: $1,200–$1,800** · **Surplus: $1,500+/mo**

Penang's capital is a UNESCO World Heritage site with possibly the best street food scene on earth. More relaxed than [KL](/cities/kuala-lumpur), with beaches and hills nearby. A strong expat community makes settling in easy, and Malaysia's excellent healthcare system means you're well covered.

### 9. [Tbilisi, Georgia](/cities/tbilisi)

**Monthly cost: $1,000–$1,600** · **Surplus: $1,700+/mo**

Georgia offers visa-free stays of up to a year for most nationalities — no paperwork required. Tbilisi has a fascinating mix of ancient and modern culture, excellent wine, and remarkably low costs. It's a hidden gem that's gaining popularity fast among the FIRE community.

### 10. [Playa del Carmen, Mexico](/cities/playa-del-carmen)

**Monthly cost: $1,800–$2,600** · **Surplus: $700+/mo**

Caribbean beach living without Caribbean island prices. The Riviera Maya offers turquoise water, cenotes, and Mayan ruins. A large international community means English is widely spoken, and healthcare options are solid with both local clinics and hospitals in nearby Cancún.

## How to Choose: The Four Factors That Matter Most

With $1 million and the 4% rule, you have $3,333/month to work with. In at least half the cities above, that leaves you with a **$1,000+ monthly surplus**. But cost isn't everything. Here's what else to weigh:

- **Visa accessibility** — Can you actually live there long-term? Some countries make it easy (Portugal, Georgia, Mexico), others require significant savings in local banks (Thailand).

- **Healthcare quality** — This becomes more important as you age. Southeast Asian hubs like [Bangkok](/cities/bangkok) and [KL](/cities/kuala-lumpur) have world-class private hospitals at affordable prices. In Latin America, quality varies more by city.

- **Tax implications** — Some countries tax worldwide income, others don't. Georgia and Malaysia, for example, have very favorable tax treatment for foreign retirees.

- **Quality of life** — Climate, safety, community, culture, and proximity to home all matter. The cheapest city isn't always the best fit.
